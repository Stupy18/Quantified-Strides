# Story 010 — Female Athlete Cycle Modifiers

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Story number** | 010 |
| **Type** | Feature |

---

## User Story

> As a **female athlete**, I want the recommendation engine to account for my menstrual cycle phase when prescribing training, so that my sessions are calibrated to my hormonal state rather than treating every week identically.

---

## Context — Current State

The readiness composite (HRV z-score, sleep readiness, TSB, subjective readiness) is cycle-blind. Late luteal HRV suppression — which is organic and progesterone-driven — is treated identically to training-load suppression, producing false rest prescriptions during a phase where the athlete could tolerate moderate work. The follicular anabolic window (peak estrogen, best adaptation stimulus) is not exploited. The `menstrual_cycles` table exists and is populated from Garmin cycle tracking data but is not consumed by any intelligence layer.

---

## New Behavior

### Phase Detection

Cycle phases are derived from the `menstrual_cycles` table. All phase boundaries are anchored to **cycle day 1 = first day of menstruation**, scaled to the athlete's **personal median cycle length** (computed from the last 6 logged cycles). If fewer than 3 cycles are logged, use 28 days as the initial default.

**Standard phase map (28-day reference cycle):**

| Phase | Days | Key physiology |
|---|---|---|
| Menstrual | 1–5 | Prostaglandins peak days 1–3 → dysmenorrhea in susceptible athletes. HRV not systematically suppressed in eumenorrheic athletes. |
| Follicular | 6–13 | Rising estrogen → anabolic signaling, peak satellite cell activity, best glycogen synthesis. Optimal adaptation window. |
| Ovulatory | 14–16 | LH surge → peak neuromuscular efficiency. Ligamentous laxity increases (2–5 mm at knee — real but modest effect). |
| Early luteal | 17–22 | Progesterone thermogenic effect: core temp +0.3–0.5°C, elevated RPE at identical HR, ventilatory drive increased. |
| Late luteal | 23–28 | Progesterone + estrogen fall. Sleep quality degrades. HRV suppression is organic (not training-derived) — distinguishable from load suppression by TSB context. |

Phase boundaries scale proportionally when personal median differs from 28 days.

---

### Modifiers Per Phase

The cycle modifier layer sits **between** `readiness_composite()` and the final prescription. It operates at two levels:

- **Prescription-level modifiers** — applied after `readiness_level` is computed; they change what the label prescribes but do not change the label itself
- **Signal-level input** — one case only: the menstrual dysmenorrhea gate feeds into the existing subjective readiness channel (not a synthetic injection)

The `readiness_level` label (`'peak'`, `'good'`, `'moderate'`, `'low'`, `'rest'`) is never directly overwritten by cycle phase alone.

---

**Menstrual (days 1–5):**

- No algorithmic intensity cap for all athletes
- Athletes with `user_profile.menstrual_dysmenorrhea = TRUE`: if `daily_readiness.overall_feel ≤ 2`, apply one-step readiness downgrade (e.g., `'good'` → `'moderate'`). Gate is active days 1–5, with intensity decay — strongest influence days 1–3, tapering to minimal by day 5
- CSP solver deprioritises novel movement patterns days 1–3: prefer exercises already in the athlete's established rotation over first-exposure to a new movement (prostaglandin-elevated inflammation increases DOMS from novel stimuli)
- No volume cuts for athletes without the dysmenorrhea flag

**Follicular (days 6–13):**

- No penalty, no restriction
- If `readiness_level ≥ 'good'` AND `strength_goal in ('hypertrophy', 'strength')`: apply **+1 working set per compound movement**, capped at +20% total session volume above baseline
- Mechanism: estrogen-enhanced satellite cell activity increases the stimulus-to-adaptation ratio; the extra set is more efficiently converted to hypertrophic adaptation during this phase
- This bonus is **suspended** when `readiness_level < 'good'` — the anabolic window requires an intact autonomic state to exploit

**Ovulatory (days 14–16):**

- Plyometrics and explosive lower-body patterns (`movement_pattern IN ('plyometric', 'explosive_lower')`) are **soft-gated**: eligible if `readiness_composite > 0.8`, but always accompanied by an awareness cue: *"Knee laxity is slightly elevated this week — prioritise landing mechanics and avoid maximal jump volume."*
- This is an awareness flag, not a hard exclusion. The evidence for ACL laxity increase is real (2–5 mm) but effect sizes in prospective injury studies are modest and confounded by training load and movement quality. Athlete is informed and chooses.
- Knee-dominant compound movements (squat, lunge, single-leg RDL): reduce loading by 10% from recent working weight

**Early luteal (days 17–22):**

- Threshold and tempo aerobic sessions: lower HR target ceiling by **3–5 bpm**
- Mechanism: progesterone's thermogenic effect causes cardiovascular drift — the same HR corresponds to higher physiological stress than established zones reflect. This is a **zone calibration offset**, not a readiness penalty. `readiness_level` is unchanged
- Affects aerobic prescription only; strength prescription unchanged

**Late luteal (days 23–28):**

- HRV suppression in this window is organic, not training-derived. **Do not mask it.** Surface it explicitly as a coaching cue: *"Your HRV is in a natural low point for this phase of your cycle. Recovery window — prioritise effort feel over zone targets this week."*
- The follicular volume bonus does not apply here
- No additional volume cuts unless `readiness_level` independently calls for them

---

### Contradictory Signal Handling

**HRV suppressed during follicular phase:**
HRV wins on safety. Follicular bonus is suspended. Surface anomaly cue: *"Your HRV is lower than typical for this cycle phase. Consider an extra recovery day. If persistent, check for illness or overtraining."*

**HRV elevated during late luteal:**
Late luteal organic-suppression cue does not fire. Prescription proceeds normally through the standard readiness pipeline.

**Ovulatory plyometric awareness cue vs. suppressed readiness:**
Awareness cue fires regardless of readiness level — it is an informational note, not a gate. If readiness is suppressed, plyometrics are already excluded by the readiness gate; the cue is suppressed too (nothing to flag if the session is already restricted).

---

### Data Quality and Missing Data

**Cycle length:** Use athlete's personal median from last 6 logged cycles. If median falls outside 21–35 days, flag as irregular: *"Your cycle data shows an irregular pattern — cycle phase modifiers may be less accurate."* Never permanently default to 28 days past the third logged cycle.

**Graceful decay when data is stale:**
- Days 0 to `(median_cycle_length + 2)`: full modifiers applied
- Days `(median_cycle_length + 3)` to `(median_cycle_length + 9)`: modifier strength decays linearly to zero
- Day `(median_cycle_length + 10)+`: modifiers disabled; surface notification: *"No recent cycle data — using baseline recommendations. Log a cycle start date to re-enable cycle-personalised insights."*

This is parameterised per athlete's actual cycle length — not a fixed 35-day cliff.

**Hormonal contraception:**
- `user_profile.hormonal_contraception BOOLEAN` — captured at onboarding
- If `TRUE`: four-phase cycle model disabled entirely. Combined oral contraceptives suppress endogenous hormonal variation; the phase model does not apply. Progesterone-only methods have variable effects — treat conservatively as combined
- User sees: *"Contraception detected — cycle-specific modifiers disabled. You can update this in Settings."*

### RED-S Risk Flag

Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport is the most clinically significant female athlete health risk that this system can detect early signals of. The system already captures the necessary signals: ATL ramp rate, sleep score trends, and cycle regularity.

**Trigger:** All three conditions simultaneously:
1. ATL ramp rate > 10% week-over-week for 3+ consecutive weeks
2. 7-day rolling sleep score slope is negative (sleep degrading)
3. Most recent two cycle lengths > 32 days (cycle lengthening — early deficiency signal)

**Response:**
- Cap recommended aerobic intensity at Zone 2 until flag clears
- Surface narrative alert: *"Multiple signals suggest possible energy deficiency. Consider reviewing your nutritional intake relative to training load. Consult a sports medicine physician if this persists."*
- This is not a diagnosis — it is an early warning. Flag clears when all three conditions are no longer simultaneously true for 7 consecutive days

### Bone Stress Fracture Risk Flag

Estrogen deficiency (from low energy availability or cycle irregularity) reduces bone mineral density and elevates stress fracture risk, particularly in the tibia and metatarsals.

**Trigger:** Both conditions simultaneously:
1. Recommended weekly running mileage increase > 15% for two consecutive weeks
2. Cycle gaps > 35 days (cycle disruption — estrogen deficiency indicator)

**Response:**
- Cap recommended weekly mileage increase at 10% (tighter than the standard 10% guideline, specifically because cycle irregularity is a bone health risk factor)
- Surface warning: *"Cycle irregularity detected alongside rapid mileage increase — bone stress fracture risk elevated. Volume increase capped at 10% this week."*

Both flags require `user_profile.biological_sex = 'female'`. Neither flag fires for male athletes.

---

## Out of Scope

- Diagnosis of any medical condition — all flags are informational and direct to a physician
- Hormonal contraception type differentiation (combined vs. progestogen-only vs. IUD) — all treated conservatively as combined in v1
- Finer hormonal sub-phase tracking (LH surge detection, estradiol levels) — Garmin data does not provide this
- Male athlete hormonal modifiers — future work
- Nutritional tracking integration (RED-S trigger uses training load + sleep + cycle as proxies only)
